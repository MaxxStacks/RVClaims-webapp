Public buyers visit the Bidder Portal and create an account. Registration includes identity verification — name, email, phone, and payment method. A bidding deposit is required before the buyer can place bids. This process filters non-serious inquiries and protects dealers from fraudulent bids.

If bidding does not reach the dealer's reserve price, the unit is not sold. It returns to the Dealer Exchange and can be pushed to the next monthly auction — same listing, same photos, no relisting required. The dealer is never forced to sell below their minimum.

All payments are processed through Stripe. The winning bidder's deposit is applied toward the purchase price. The remaining balance is collected through Stripe before delivery is coordinated. DS360 manages the entire payment flow — the dealer receives funds after the transaction is confirmed complete.

Every listing includes comprehensive photo galleries and full specifications. For buyers who want an in-person inspection, arrangements can be made through DS360 before the auction closes — subject to dealer availability and location. Remote buyers rely on the documented condition report and photo evidence.

Not until the transaction closes. During the auction, DS360 acts as the intermediary. The dealer's identity is protected throughout the bidding process. After the winning bid is confirmed and escrow is secured, DS360 facilitates the introduction for delivery coordination.
