Your Day on DS360

Morning to Close — What Happens at Each Step

This is a typical day for a dealer running their operation through the DS360 portal.

8:00 AM

Morning Dashboard Check

Log in and scan your dashboard. See which claims moved overnight — approvals, parts shipped, deadlines approaching. Check notifications for any manufacturer responses that came in after hours. Review today's priority list automatically sorted by urgency.

9:00 AM

New Claim Submission

A unit came in yesterday with a water heater failure. Open the unit dashboard by VIN, upload inspection photos, and the AI Document Scanner reads the warranty certificate and auto-populates coverage details. AI suggests FRC codes and generates a fail-proof description. Submit to DS360 for processing.

10:30 AM

Parts Status Update

Check the Parts & Components tracker. Two parts ordered last week show "shipped" — ETAs are visible. A third part is sourced from an alternative supplier at a lower cost. Update the client through the Customer Portal messaging — they see the status change in real time.

12:00 PM

F&I Product Sale

A client who purchased a unit last week received their AI F&I Presenter link. They accepted Extended Warranty and Appearance Protection overnight. The accepted products auto-synced to your portal — no manual entry. Revenue logged. Cashback tier updated.

2:00 PM

TechFlow Service Check

An approved warranty claim auto-generated a TechFlow work order this morning. Your service manager assigned it to a technician. The tech logged 3.5 hours — those hours are already synced back to the claim invoice. Zero manual time entry.

3:30 PM

Marketplace Activity

A travel trailer has been on your lot for 75 days. You pushed it to the Dealer Exchange last week. Today, a dealer 400KM away inquired. DS360 initiated the escrow process. You might move this unit by end of week without a single walk-in.

5:00 PM

End-of-Day Review

Check billing summary — claims submitted, products sold, parts ordered, marketplace transactions pending. Review any deadline alerts for tomorrow. Log out knowing that the AI F&I Presenter is still presenting products to clients who received their links today — working after hours so you don't have to.

Role-Based Views

Dealer Owner VERSUS Dealer Staff — Different Views, Same Portal

Not everyone at your dealership needs to see everything. DS360's RBAC system gives the owner full access while restricting staff to operational tasks only. Both roles log into the same portal — they just see different things.

Dealer Owner Sees

Claims, units, billing, invoices, payment history, staff management, F&I product access, marketplace listings, TechFlow, reporting, and cashback tier status

Dealer Staff Sees

Claims and units for their dealership — operational access only. No billing, no invoices, no staff management, no admin configuration

Same Login, Different Experience

Staff members are added through the owner's dashboard with their role assigned. No separate portal, no separate URL — just filtered access
