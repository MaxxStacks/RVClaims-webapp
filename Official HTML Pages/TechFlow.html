How TechFlow Works

One Workflow. Zero Handoff Gaps.

From claim approval to completed repair — every step connected, every hour tracked.

Claim Approved Automatic

The manufacturer approves the warranty claim. DS360 captures the repair scope, authorized labor hours, and part requirements. TechFlow picks up immediately.

Work Order Generated Automatic

TechFlow creates a work order containing the approved repair details — what needs to be done, what parts are authorized, how many labor hours are approved, and the claim reference number.

Technician Assigned Your Team

Your service manager assigns the work order to the appropriate technician based on skill set, availability, and workload. The technician sees the full repair scope on their dashboard.

Labor Hours Tracked Automatic

As the technician works, labor hours are tracked against the work order. Start, pause, complete — every minute is documented and tied to the approved claim.

Hours Sync to Invoice Automatic

Completed labor hours flow directly into the claim invoice alongside parts costs. The invoice is accurate, complete, and ready for manufacturer payout — no manual entry, no missed hours.

"If a technician works on a warranty repair and the hours are not on the invoice, your dealership just donated that labor for free."

TechFlow is an add-on service available on top of any DS360 dealer plan. It is not included by default because not every dealer needs service department management — but for those who do, adding it takes minutes.

TechFlow is designed to work with the DS360 claims workflow — the auto-generated work orders and invoice sync depend on claims data. It can function for general service work, but the real power is the claims integration.

TechFlow supports both warranty and non-warranty work orders. Customer-pay repairs, maintenance work, and internal jobs can all be created and tracked — the difference is that warranty work orders auto-generate from approved claims.

TechFlow pricing is flexible — details are available on the Pricing page. The system is built to scale from a single-tech operation to a multi-bay dealership with dozens of technicians.

When the DS360 Mobile App launches, technicians will be able to view work orders, clock hours, and update repair status directly from the service bay — no need to walk to a desktop computer.
