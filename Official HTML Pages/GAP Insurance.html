Why It Sells

The Product Your Client Understands Instantly

Most F&I products require an explanation. GAP Insurance requires one question: "If your RV is totaled tomorrow and insurance only pays $54,000 but you owe $72,000 — who pays the $18,000 difference?" The answer is either the client or GAP Insurance. There is no third option.

RVs are particularly vulnerable to GAP exposure because they depreciate faster than the loan amortizes — especially with long-term financing. A 15-year loan on a new fifth wheel can create a GAP exposure window that lasts 6 to 8 years.

GAP Insurance is not a luxury for RV buyers. It is a financial necessity for anyone financing a depreciating asset over a long term.

Yes. GAP exposure exists on any financed unit — new or pre-owned. Used units often carry significant negative equity from day one if the client finances more than the unit's current market value. GAP Insurance covers both scenarios.

A GAP claim is triggered when the unit is declared a total loss by the insurance company — due to collision, weather damage, fire, or theft where the unit is not recovered. The insurance company pays the actual cash value. GAP covers the remaining loan balance.

GAP covers the difference between the insurance payout (actual cash value) and the outstanding loan balance, up to the policy limits. Specific coverage limits and terms are outlined in the policy certificate provided to the client.

DS360 handles the claim end to end — working with the client's insurance company to confirm the total loss payout, verifying the outstanding loan balance with the lender, calculating the gap amount, and facilitating the payout. Your dealership is kept informed but does not need to manage the process.
