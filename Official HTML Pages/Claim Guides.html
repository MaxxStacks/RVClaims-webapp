What Is a DAF Claim?

A DAF (Damage at Factory) claim covers defects or damage that occurred during manufacturing or transit before the unit reached the dealership. These are identified during the dealer’s initial receiving inspection.

When to File

DAF claims must be filed within the manufacturer’s DAF window — typically 7 to 30 days from receiving the unit. Missing this deadline is the single most common reason DAF claims are denied. DS360 tracks receiving dates and sends automated deadline alerts before expiration.

Photo Requirements

  • Wide shot showing the full affected area in context
  • Close-up of the specific defect with a ruler or reference object for scale
  • VIN plate photo for verification
  • Photos taken in natural light — no flash, no dark service bays

FRC Code Strategy

DAF claims use manufacturer-specific FRC codes. Jayco, Forest River, Heartland, Keystone, Columbia NW, and Midwest Auto each have unique code sets. DS360’s AI suggests the correct FRC code based on the defect description and photo analysis. Always verify the AI suggestion against the manufacturer’s current code list — codes are updated quarterly.

Description Template

“During receiving inspection on [DATE], [DEFECT TYPE] was identified on [COMPONENT]. The damage is consistent with [factory assembly / transit handling]. Photos attached showing [specific detail]. Unit has not been delivered to client. Requesting authorization for [repair type].”

Common Denial Triggers

  • Filing after the DAF deadline expires
  • Photos taken after the unit was delivered to a client
  • Description does not specify factory or transit origin
  • Missing VIN verification photo

What Is a PDI Claim?

A PDI claim covers issues identified during the pre-delivery inspection — the dealer’s quality check before handing the unit to the client. PDI claims catch manufacturing defects that were not visible during the initial receiving inspection.

When to File

PDI claims are filed after the full PDI checklist is completed but before the unit is delivered to the client. Timing varies by manufacturer but is typically 15 to 45 days from the PDI date. DS360 auto-tracks PDI dates and flags approaching deadlines.

PDI Checklist Integration

DS360 integrates the PDI checklist directly into the unit dashboard. As your technician completes each inspection point, any defect found can be photographed, described, and queued for claim submission in real time — no separate paperwork required.

Key Differences from DAF

  • DAF = damage from factory or transit. PDI = defects found during quality inspection.
  • PDI claims often cover functional issues (appliances, plumbing, electrical) not just cosmetic damage
  • PDI requires proof that the full inspection was completed per manufacturer standards

The DS360 Warranty Claim Workflow

Warranty claims follow a structured lifecycle: Inspect → FRC Lines → Submit → Approve/Deny → Parts → Repair → Invoice → Payment → Close. DS360 tracks every claim through every stage with real-time status updates visible to both the dealer and the operator.

FRC Lines — Multiple Per Claim

Each warranty claim can have multiple FRC lines. Each line represents a separate repair item, and each is approved or denied individually by the manufacturer. This is where dealers lose money — missing a valid labor line on a claim means lost revenue that can never be recovered. DS360’s AI analyzes the inspection data and suggests FRC lines the dealer may have missed.

Labor Line Maximization

  • Always include diagnostic time as a separate line
  • Travel time for mobile/on-site repairs is billable
  • Component removal and reinstallation are separate labor operations
  • If a repair requires accessing a component behind another (e.g., removing a wall panel to reach plumbing), both are billable

Manufacturer Assignment

The manufacturer assigns the claim number and preauthorization number — not the dealer. DS360 is a tracking and management portal. Actual claim submissions go through manufacturer portals. DS360 prepares the optimized submission package and tracks the response.

How Extended Warranty Claims Differ

Extended warranty claims are processed through DS360 directly — not through manufacturer warranty portals. This gives DS360 full control over approval timelines and payout speed. Coverage is based on the client’s plan tier (Basic, Standard, or Premium) and the specific components covered under each tier.

Coverage Verification

Before submitting an extended warranty claim, DS360 verifies the client’s coverage tier, plan effective dates, and whether the specific component is covered. This verification is automatic — when a dealer creates a claim against a VIN with an active extended warranty, the system checks coverage before the claim is even assigned FRC codes.

Payout Speed

Because DS360 manages extended warranty claims internally, payout timelines are significantly faster than manufacturer warranty claims. Target: approval within 48 hours, payout within 7 business days of completed repair documentation.

Why Photos Cause Denials

Manufacturers deny claims when photos are dark, blurry, taken from too far away, or fail to show the defect clearly. DS360’s AI Document Scanner analyzes photo quality before submission and flags images that are likely to cause issues.

The 4-Photo Rule

  • Context shot: Full unit exterior showing where the defect is located
  • Area shot: The affected area from 3-4 feet showing surrounding context
  • Detail shot: Close-up of the specific defect with scale reference (ruler, coin, hand)
  • VIN shot: Clear photo of the VIN plate for verification

Technical Requirements

  • Natural light whenever possible — no camera flash
  • Minimum 2MP resolution — most modern phones exceed this
  • No filters, no edits, no annotations on photos
  • Landscape orientation for context shots, portrait for detail shots

What Are FRC Codes?

FRC (Failure/Repair Condition) codes are manufacturer-specific codes that categorize the type of defect and repair being claimed. Using the correct FRC code is critical — an incorrect code can result in denial even if the repair itself is valid.

Manufacturer Code Sets

DS360 processes claims for 6 manufacturers, each with unique FRC code structures:

  • Jayco: Alphanumeric codes organized by system (electrical, plumbing, structural, cosmetic)
  • Forest River: Numeric codes with sub-categories by component group
  • Heartland: Code structure similar to Forest River with additional labor classification
  • Keystone: Detailed codes with specific component-level granularity
  • Columbia NW: Simplified code set with broader categories
  • Midwest Auto: Custom code structure requiring specific description formats

AI-Assisted Code Matching

DS360’s AI analyzes the defect description and photos to suggest the most accurate FRC code. The AI cross-references against historical approval data — codes that have been approved for similar defects in the past are weighted higher. The specialist always verifies before submission.

The Denials That Cost Dealers the Most

  • 1. Expired deadline: Filed after the manufacturer’s submission window closed. DS360 prevents this with automated deadline tracking and alerts.
  • 2. Poor photos: Dark, blurry, or missing context. DS360 AI flags low-quality images before submission.
  • 3. Wrong FRC code: Mismatched code for the defect type. DS360 AI suggests the correct code from historical data.
  • 4. Weak description: Generic language that does not specify the defect, cause, or requested repair. Use the description templates in each guide.
  • 5. Missing labor lines: Diagnostic time, removal/reinstallation, and travel time omitted. DS360 AI flags missing billable lines.
  • 6. No VIN verification: Missing VIN plate photo. Required on every submission.
  • 7. Client delivery before DAF filing: DAF claims filed after the unit was delivered to the client are automatically denied.
  • 8. Duplicate submission: Claim already exists for the same VIN and defect. DS360 checks for duplicates before submission.
  • 9. Non-covered component: Claiming a component not covered under the unit’s warranty or plan tier. DS360 verifies coverage before submission.
  • 10. Incomplete repair documentation: Repair completed but invoice, labor hours, or parts receipts not uploaded. DS360 tracks required documents per claim.

Where Dealers Lose Labor Revenue

The average dealer misses 1.5 to 3 billable labor lines per warranty claim. Over 50+ claims per month, that is tens of thousands of dollars in unbilled work annually. DS360’s AI analyzes each claim and flags labor lines the dealer may have missed.

Commonly Missed Labor Lines

  • Diagnostic time: Time spent identifying the root cause before repair begins — always billable as a separate line
  • Access labor: Removing panels, walls, or components to reach the actual repair area
  • Reinstallation labor: Putting everything back after the repair — separate from the repair itself
  • Testing and verification: Time spent verifying the repair is complete (water testing, electrical testing, operational checks)
  • Travel time: For on-site or mobile repairs, travel to the client location is billable
  • Cleanup and disposal: Removing old parts, cleaning the work area — billable on larger jobs

TechFlow Integration

When TechFlow is active, technician labor hours are logged directly in the system and automatically synced to the claim invoice. This eliminates the manual step where labor lines get lost — if the tech logged it, it appears on the claim.
