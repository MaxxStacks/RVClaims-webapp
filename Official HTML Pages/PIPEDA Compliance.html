1. Our Commitment to PIPEDA

DealerSuite360 is committed to full compliance with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) — Canada’s federal privacy law governing the collection, use, and disclosure of personal information in the course of commercial activity.

PIPEDA compliance is not an afterthought at DS360. It is built into the platform architecture, our data handling practices, and our organizational culture. Every feature, every data flow, and every third-party integration is evaluated against PIPEDA requirements before deployment.

Most competitor platforms in the RV dealer space do not have a dedicated PIPEDA compliance page. We do, because we believe transparency about data practices is a fundamental part of earning dealer trust.

2. The 10 Fair Information Principles

PIPEDA is built on 10 fair information principles. Here is how DS360 addresses each one:

1. Accountability

DS360 has designated a Privacy Officer responsible for compliance with PIPEDA. Our Privacy Officer oversees all data handling practices, responds to access requests, and ensures ongoing compliance as the platform evolves.

2. Identifying Purposes

We identify the purpose for collecting personal information at or before the time of collection. Every data field in the platform has a documented purpose tied to a specific service function — claims processing, F&I product delivery, marketplace transactions, or client communication.

3. Consent

We obtain meaningful consent before collecting, using, or disclosing personal information. Consent mechanisms are built into account registration, client portal invitations, and bidder verification. Users can withdraw consent at any time, subject to legal and contractual obligations.

4. Limiting Collection

We collect only the personal information necessary for the identified purposes. The platform does not collect extraneous data. Each data field serves a specific operational function.

5. Limiting Use, Disclosure, and Retention

Personal information is used only for the purpose it was collected. It is not disclosed to third parties except as described in our Privacy Policy. Data retention periods are defined by service type and legal requirements.

6. Accuracy

We maintain accurate, complete, and up-to-date information. Users can update their information through the platform at any time. Claims data is verified against manufacturer records for accuracy.

7. Safeguards

DS360 protects personal information with enterprise-grade security measures: 256-bit AES encryption, role-based access control (RBAC), multi-tenant database isolation, JWT authentication, and SOC 2 ready infrastructure. Platform monitoring operates 24/7/365.

8. Openness

Our privacy practices are documented in our Privacy Policy, this PIPEDA Compliance page, and our Cookie Policy. These documents are publicly accessible and updated with every material change.

9. Individual Access

Users can request access to the personal information DS360 holds about them. We respond to access requests within 30 days. Users can also request correction of inaccurate information.

10. Challenging Compliance

Users who believe their privacy rights have been violated can file a complaint with our Privacy Officer. If the complaint is not resolved satisfactorily, users may escalate to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada.

3. Data Handling Practices

DS360 handles three categories of personal information in the context of dealer operations:

  • Dealer Data: Business information, staff accounts, billing details, and operational records. Collected directly from the dealer during onboarding and ongoing use.
  • Client Data: Customer information collected on behalf of dealers — names, contact details, VINs, warranty records, claim submissions, and communication logs. Processed to deliver services requested by the dealer.
  • Bidder Data: Public auction participant information including identity verification, payment details, and transaction records. Collected to facilitate secure marketplace transactions.

All data is stored on secure servers located in North America. Where third-party processors are involved (Stripe, Anthropic, Railway), contractual safeguards ensure PIPEDA-equivalent protection.

4. Individual Access Rights

Under PIPEDA, individuals have the right to:

  • Request access to personal information held by DS360
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Withdraw consent for data collection, use, or disclosure (subject to legal/contractual obligations)
  • File a complaint about DS360’s privacy practices

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Privacy Officer at privacy@dealersuite360.com. We respond within 30 days.
