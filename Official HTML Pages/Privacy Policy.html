1. Overview

DealerSuite360 ("DS360," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and personal information of our users, including dealers, dealer staff, clients, bidders, and website visitors. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, disclose, and protect your personal information when you use our platform, services, and website.

DS360 operates in compliance with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and applicable Canadian provincial privacy legislation. We are committed to the ten fair information principles outlined in PIPEDA: accountability, identifying purposes, consent, limiting collection, limiting use, accuracy, safeguards, openness, individual access, and challenging compliance.

By using the DS360 platform, services, or website, you consent to the collection, use, and disclosure of your personal information as described in this policy.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal information:

Dealer and Staff Information:

  • Business name, address, phone number, and email address
  • Contact person name, role, and direct contact information
  • Staff names, email addresses, and role assignments
  • Business registration and licensing information
  • Billing and payment information

Client Information (collected on behalf of dealers):

  • Name, email address, phone number
  • Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) and unit details
  • Warranty and coverage information
  • Claim submissions including photographs and descriptions
  • Communication records with dealers through the platform

Bidder Information:

  • Name, email address, phone number
  • Identity verification information
  • Payment and deposit information via Stripe
  • Bidding history and transaction records

Website Visitor Information:

  • IP address, browser type, device information
  • Pages visited, time spent, and referral source
  • Information submitted through contact forms

3. How We Use Your Information

We use personal information for the following purposes:

  • Providing and operating the DS360 platform and services
  • Processing warranty claims, F&I products, and marketplace transactions
  • Managing dealer accounts, billing, and subscriptions
  • Facilitating communication between dealers and clients through the platform
  • Processing payments and escrow transactions through Stripe
  • Verifying bidder identity for auction participation
  • Sending service-related notifications, alerts, and updates
  • Improving platform functionality and user experience
  • Complying with legal obligations and regulatory requirements
  • Preventing fraud, abuse, and unauthorized access

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

4. Disclosure of Information

We may disclose personal information in the following circumstances:

  • To dealers: Client information necessary to fulfill services, process claims, and manage the dealer-client relationship
  • To service providers: Third-party providers who assist in operating the platform (hosting, payment processing, email delivery) under contractual obligations to protect your information
  • To payment processors: Stripe receives payment and transaction information necessary to process payments and manage escrow
  • To AI service providers: Anthropic receives document and claim data necessary to power AI features, subject to data processing agreements
  • As required by law: When disclosure is required by Canadian federal or provincial law, court order, or regulatory requirement
  • For safety: When necessary to protect the rights, safety, or property of DS360, our users, or the public

In marketplace transactions, dealer identity is protected until both parties confirm the transaction. Bidder information is shared with the selling dealer only after a winning bid is confirmed and escrow is secured.

5. Data Storage & Security

DS360 implements enterprise-grade security measures to protect personal information:

  • Encryption: 256-bit AES encryption for all data at rest and in transit
  • Access control: Role-based access control (RBAC) with four permission tiers ensuring users access only the data necessary for their role
  • Multi-tenant isolation: Database-level tenant separation ensures no cross-dealer data access
  • Authentication: JWT token authentication with automatic rotation
  • Infrastructure: SOC 2 ready infrastructure with 99.9% uptime SLA
  • Monitoring: 24/7/365 platform monitoring with automated threat detection

Personal information is stored on secure servers located in North America. Where data is processed by third-party service providers, we ensure appropriate contractual safeguards are in place to protect your information in accordance with PIPEDA and applicable privacy legislation in both Canada and the United States.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law:

  • Active accounts: Information is retained for the duration of the account relationship
  • Closed accounts: Information is retained for a reasonable period after account closure to comply with legal, tax, and audit requirements
  • Claims data: Claim records are retained for the duration required by manufacturer agreements and applicable warranty legislation
  • Transaction records: Financial and transaction records are retained as required by Canadian tax law
  • Website visitor data: Analytics and visitor data is retained for a maximum of 24 months

When personal information is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymized in accordance with our data destruction procedures.

7. Your Rights Under PIPEDA

Under PIPEDA, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: You may request access to the personal information we hold about you
  • Correction: You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal information
  • Withdrawal of consent: You may withdraw consent for the collection, use, or disclosure of your personal information, subject to legal or contractual restrictions
  • Complaint: You may file a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada if you believe your privacy rights have been violated

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Privacy Officer using the contact information provided at the end of this policy. We will respond to access and correction requests within 30 days.

8. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

DS360 uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website and platform:

  • Essential cookies: Required for platform functionality — authentication, session management, and security. These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: Used to understand how visitors interact with our website and platform to improve the user experience. Can be disabled.
  • Marketing cookies: Used for targeted advertising and remarketing campaigns through Google Ads, Outbrain, Taboola, and AdRoll. These cookies track browsing behavior to serve relevant ads across the web. Can be disabled.

You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings. Disabling essential cookies may affect platform functionality.

9. Third-Party Services

DS360 integrates with the following third-party services that may collect or process personal information:

  • Maxx Stacks: Technology partner providing MSIL — the proprietary engine and AI intelligence layer that powers all DS360 tools. Maxx Stacks processes operational data necessary for platform functionality under a lifetime exclusivity agreement and data processing terms.
  • Stripe: Payment processing, escrow management, and financial transactions. Stripe's privacy policy governs their handling of payment data.
  • Anthropic: AI processing for claims optimization, document scanning, and F&I presentation. Data shared with Anthropic is subject to data processing agreements.
  • Railway: Cloud hosting and infrastructure. Data is stored on Railway's infrastructure with enterprise-grade security.
  • Google: Analytics (Google Analytics), search indexing (Google Search Console), Places API for dealer directory, and advertising services (Google Ads) for paid search, display, and remarketing campaigns. Google's privacy policy governs their handling of analytics and advertising data.
  • Outbrain: Content discovery and native advertising platform used for marketing campaigns and audience targeting. Outbrain may collect browsing behavior data for ad delivery and optimization.
  • Taboola: Content recommendation and native advertising platform used for marketing campaigns and audience reach. Taboola may collect browsing behavior data for ad personalization and delivery.
  • AdRoll: Retargeting and display advertising platform used to serve remarketing ads to website visitors across the web. AdRoll may collect cookie and browsing data to identify and retarget visitors who have previously interacted with DS360.

We require all third-party service providers to implement appropriate security measures and process personal information only for the purposes specified in our agreements.

10. Children's Privacy

DS360 is a business-to-business platform designed for dealership operations. Our services are not directed at individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected information from a minor, we will take steps to delete that information promptly.

11. Changes to This Policy

DS360 may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or business operations. When we make material changes, we will notify affected users through the platform, email, or a prominent notice on our website. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy indicates when the most recent revision was made.

Continued use of the DS360 platform or services after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the revised terms.
