# Getting Started with DealerSuite360

Welcome to DealerSuite360. This guide walks you through your first week on the platform — from account setup to submitting your first claim.

## Step 1: Complete Your Dealership Profile

Before filing your first claim, make sure your dealership profile is complete. Go to **Settings** → **Dealership Profile** and confirm:

- Dealership legal name (must match manufacturer portal records)
- Physical address and province/state
- Primary contact phone number
- Supported manufacturers (tell your Account Manager which manufacturer portals you work with)

This information is used by your operator team when submitting claims to manufacturer portals on your behalf.

## Step 2: Add Your First Unit

Every claim in DealerSuite360 is tied to a unit (an RV). Add your units before creating claims.

Go to **My Units** → **Add Unit**:

1. Enter the 17-character VIN
2. DealerSuite360 will attempt to auto-populate the year, make, model, and type from our database
3. If the VIN isn't found, select the manufacturer from the dropdown and fill in the details manually
4. Set the unit type (Travel Trailer, Fifth Wheel, Class A, etc.)
5. Save the unit

You don't need to add all your units at once. Add them as you create claims.

## Step 3: Create Your First Claim

Go to **My Claims** → **New Claim**:

1. **Select the unit** — search by VIN or unit name
2. **Select the claim type** — DAF, PDI, Warranty, Extended Warranty, or Insurance
3. **Add claim lines** — each repair is a separate line item. For each line:
   - Describe the problem in plain language (the system will suggest FRC codes)
   - Complete the 3C description: Complaint, Cause, and Correct
   - Upload at minimum 2 photos: a close-up of the defect and a wider context shot
4. **Save as Draft** until everything is complete

## Step 4: Review and Submit to Your Operator

When all lines have complete 3C descriptions and photos, submit the claim to your operator team:

- **Submit Claim** sends your claim to DealerSuite360 operators for review
- Operators review your photos and FRC code suggestions before submitting to the manufacturer
- If photos are insufficient, you'll receive a notification with specific feedback

You are NOT submitting directly to the manufacturer — DealerSuite360 handles that step on your behalf.

## Step 5: Track Your Claim

After submission, track your claim's progress in **My Claims**:

- **Submitted** — Sent to operators, under review
- **With Manufacturer** — Operators have submitted to the manufacturer portal
- **Approved** — Manufacturer has approved one or more lines
- **Denied** — One or more lines were denied (review denial reasons in the claim detail)

You'll receive in-app notifications when claim status changes.

## Step 6: Invite Your Team

If you have technicians or service advisors who will be creating claims, invite them as Dealer Staff:

Go to **Staff** → **Invite Staff Member** → Enter their name and email address → **Send Invitation**

They'll receive an email with a link to set up their account. Staff members can create and manage claims but cannot access billing.

## Step 7: Set Up Billing

Your subscription was set up during onboarding. To review your plan or add a payment method:

Go to **Billing** → **Subscription** to view your current plan and fees.

If you're on Plan B (pre-funded wallet), top up your wallet before submitting your first claim so processing fees can be deducted automatically.

## Need Help?

Use the **DS360 Assist** button (bottom right of any page) to:
- Search this knowledge base
- Chat with a support agent
- Request a screen share session with our team
- Email your Account Manager

For urgent issues, use the **Live Chat** escalation option in DS360 Assist.
