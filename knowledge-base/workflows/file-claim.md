# How to File a Warranty Claim

DealerSuite360 is a claim tracking and management platform. You submit your claim information here, and our operator team submits the actual claim on the manufacturer's portal on your behalf.

## Step 1: Select the Unit

Navigate to **Claims** → **New Claim**, or go to your unit detail page and click **New Claim**. Search for the unit by VIN or stock number.

If the unit doesn't exist yet, you must create it first. See "How to Create a New Unit."

## Step 2: Choose the Claim Type

- **DAF (Dealer Authorization Form)** — Used when a new unit arrives at your dealership. Documents any damage or defects found upon delivery.
- **PDI (Pre-Delivery Inspection)** — Filed before handing the RV to a customer. Covers final inspection issues.
- **Warranty** — Customer-reported issues during the manufacturer's active warranty period.
- **Extended Warranty** — Issues covered by an aftermarket extended service plan after the OEM warranty expires.
- **Insurance** — Collision, weather damage, theft, or liability claims.

## Step 3: Add Claim Lines (FRC Items)

Each defect or issue is a separate claim line. For every line you must provide:

**3C Description** — This is the industry-standard format:
- **Complaint** — What the customer reported ("Water leaking from the roof vent")
- **Cause** — What you found upon inspection ("Sealant around vent cover cracked and separated")
- **Correct** — What repair was performed ("Removed vent cover, cleaned surface, applied new butyl tape and sealant, reinstalled")

**FRC Code** — The manufacturer's Flat Rate Code for this type of repair. These codes are manufacturer-specific. If you don't know the exact code, describe the issue and our AI will suggest the best match. Your operator team will confirm the code before submission.

**Labor Hours Requested** — How many hours the repair took or will take, based on the FRC flat rate schedule.

## Step 4: Upload Photos Per Line

Photos are required **per FRC line** — not just once per claim. Each line needs:
- **Close-up photo** — Clear shot of the defect at issue
- **Context photo** — Wider shot showing where the issue is on the unit

Photos must be sharp, well-lit, and clearly show the problem. Poor photo quality is the #1 reason claims are delayed or denied. Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, HEIC. Max size: 10MB per photo.

## Step 5: Submit to Operator

Once all lines have descriptions and photos, click **Submit to Operator**. The claim status changes to **Submitted**.

Our operator team will:
1. Review all FRC lines and photos
2. Confirm FRC codes with our database
3. Submit on the manufacturer's portal
4. Log the manufacturer-assigned Claim # and Preauth #

## What Preauth# and Claim# Mean

- **Preauth #** — Manufacturer's pre-authorization number. Issued when the repair is approved before work begins. Required for warranty repairs.
- **Claim #** — Manufacturer's tracking number for the submitted claim. Used for all follow-up communication.

You do not control these numbers — the manufacturer assigns them and our operators log them into your portal.

## Tracking Your Claim

After submission, check your claim status on the **Claims** page. You'll receive in-app notifications when status changes. See "Understanding Claim Statuses" for a full explanation of each status.
