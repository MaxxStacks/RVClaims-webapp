# How to Add a New Client

Clients are your RV customers — the people who purchase units from your dealership. Adding a client connects them to their RV and gives them access to the Customer Portal where they can track their unit's warranty, claims, and service history.

## Adding a Client

1. Navigate to **Clients** (or **Customers**) in the sidebar
2. Click **Add Client** or **Invite Customer**
3. Fill in the client details:
   - **First Name** and **Last Name** (required)
   - **Email Address** (required — used for their portal login)
   - **Phone Number** (optional)
   - **Address** (optional)
4. Select the unit(s) to associate with this client
5. Click **Save** or **Send Invitation**

If you choose **Send Invitation**, the client receives an email inviting them to create their Customer Portal account.

## Linking a Client to a Unit

Every client should be linked to at least one unit. This connection:
- Allows the client to see their unit's warranty status in their portal
- Lets them view active and historical claims on their unit
- Allows them to submit service requests and support tickets

To link a client to a unit: go to the unit detail page → **Client** tab → **Assign Client**.

## What the Client Portal Shows

Once a client logs into their Customer Portal, they can see:
- Their unit's year, make, model, and VIN
- Warranty coverage details and expiration dates
- Status of any open or closed warranty claims
- Documents related to their purchase
- Protection plans they've enrolled in (if applicable)
- Support tickets they've filed

Clients **cannot** see your business financials, claim processing fees, or operator notes.

## Managing Client Access

- **Deactivate a client** — Removes their portal access without deleting their records. Use this if a client has sold their unit.
- **Transfer a unit** — If a unit changes ownership, you can reassign it to a new client.
- **Resend invitation** — If a client didn't receive or lost their invitation email, use the resend option from their profile.

## Privacy Considerations

Client data in DealerSuite360 is subject to PIPEDA (Canada) and applicable US privacy regulations. Only share client information within your dealership. The platform maintains full audit logs of who accessed client records.
