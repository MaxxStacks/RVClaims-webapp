# Photo Requirements for Claims

Photos are the single most important factor in getting warranty claims approved quickly. Poor photos are the #1 cause of claim delays and denials. DealerSuite360's photo quality system helps ensure your photos meet manufacturer standards.

## Why Photos Matter

Manufacturers use photos to verify that:
- The damage or defect actually exists
- The repair was performed correctly
- The defect is a manufacturing issue (not customer damage)
- The claimed repair area matches the FRC code submitted

Without adequate photos, manufacturers will request additional documentation — delaying your payment by weeks.

## Photo Requirements Per Line Item

Photos are required **per FRC line** — every single claim line needs its own photos. A claim with 5 FRC lines needs at minimum 10 photos (2 per line):

### 1. Close-Up Photo
A sharp, well-lit photo taken 30–60cm from the defect. The problem must be clearly visible. If it's a crack, the crack must be visible. If it's delamination, the bubble or separation must be clear.

### 2. Context Photo
A wider shot showing where the issue is located on the unit. Step back 2–3 metres. This helps the manufacturer understand the location and scale of the defect.

### Additional Photos (When Required)
- **Before/After photos** — Required for some repair types. Show the defect before work, and the completed repair after.
- **Repair invoice photo** — For parts claims, a photo of the supplier invoice may be required.
- **Serial number photo** — For appliance claims, photograph the appliance serial number plate.

## Technical Specifications

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| File formats | JPG, JPEG, PNG, HEIC |
| Maximum file size | 10MB per photo |
| Minimum resolution | 1024×768 pixels |
| Orientation | Landscape preferred |

## Common Photo Mistakes to Avoid

**Too dark** — Take photos in good lighting or use your phone's flash. Dark photos look like you're hiding something.

**Too blurry** — Hold your phone steady. Tap the defect area on your screen to focus before taking the shot.

**Wrong area** — Make sure you're photographing the actual defect, not nearby areas.

**Too far away** — The close-up must actually be close up. If you can't see the damage clearly in the thumbnail, it won't pass review.

**Missing context** — Always include at least one wider shot so reviewers understand where on the RV the repair was made.

## Photos vs. Documents

DealerSuite360 tracks two types of files per claim:
- **Photos** — Visual documentation of defects and repairs (required per FRC line)
- **Documents** — Supplier invoices, customer complaint forms, manufacturer correspondence, etc.

Upload documents separately using the **Documents** tab on the claim detail page.

## What Happens After Upload

Your photos are reviewed by your operator team before submission. If a photo doesn't meet quality standards, you'll receive a notification with specific feedback on what to replace. This review happens before the claim is submitted to the manufacturer — protecting your approval rate.
