# FRC Codes by Manufacturer

FRC (Flat Rate Code) codes are manufacturer-specific codes that identify repair types and the standard labor hours allocated for each repair. They are not universal — a code used for Jayco is completely different from a Forest River code for the same repair.

## What FRC Codes Are

Every warranty repair is billed using an FRC code from the manufacturer's flat rate schedule. Each code specifies:
- **The type of repair** (e.g., slide mechanism adjustment, roof reseal, appliance replacement)
- **Standard labor hours** (the fixed time the manufacturer will pay for, regardless of how long it actually takes)
- **Category** (electrical, plumbing, structural, appliances, etc.)

When you describe a repair in DealerSuite360, our AI assistant suggests the appropriate FRC code for your manufacturer. Our operator team confirms or adjusts the code before submission.

## Supported Manufacturers

### Jayco
Jayco uses a proprietary FRC system through their dealer portal (J-Warranty). Codes are formatted as alphanumeric sequences. Jayco has among the most detailed flat rate schedules in the industry, with specific codes for each slide type, roof construction, and appliance brand.

### Forest River
Forest River's FRC system covers their extensive brand family (Palomino, Coachmen, Prime Time, etc.). Codes are formatted differently per sub-brand. Our database includes Forest River's complete flat rate schedule and is updated quarterly.

### Heartland
Heartland (now part of Thor Industries) uses a unified FRC system for all Heartland brands (Big Country, Cyclone, Sundance, etc.). Codes are standardized within the Heartland family.

### Keystone
Keystone RV (also Thor Industries) has its own distinct FRC system covering Cougar, Montana, Sprinter, Passport, and other Keystone brands. Labor hours differ from Heartland even for similar repairs.

### Columbia Northwest (Columbia NW)
Columbia NW is a smaller Canadian manufacturer with a more concise FRC schedule. Their codes and labor times reflect their product line (Sabre, Cascade).

### Midwest Auto
Midwest Auto FRC codes are specific to their cargo trailer and specialty RV product lines. Our operator team maintains direct contact with Midwest Auto for code verification.

## When Codes Can't Be Found

If a repair is unusual or involves a newly released model, the standard FRC database may not have a match. In these cases, your operator will contact the manufacturer directly for a code assignment or work with you to find the closest applicable code.
