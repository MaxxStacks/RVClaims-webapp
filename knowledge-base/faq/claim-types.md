# Claim Types Explained

DealerSuite360 supports five distinct claim types, each covering a different stage of the RV's lifecycle or a different type of event.

## DAF — Dealer Authorization Form

**When to use:** When a new unit arrives at your dealership from the manufacturer.

A DAF claim documents any damage, defects, or missing items found when you take delivery of a unit from the factory or distribution center. This is your first line of protection — it establishes that any damage existed before the unit was in your care.

**Examples:** Dents or scratches from transit, missing awning hardware, water intrusion in a slide room, cracked exterior panels.

**Key requirement:** Must be filed within a short window after receiving the unit (typically 30 days, but check your manufacturer agreement). Delayed DAF claims are frequently denied.

## PDI — Pre-Delivery Inspection

**When to use:** Before handing the RV over to the customer.

A PDI claim covers the final inspection process before a unit is delivered to a buyer. It ensures all systems are working correctly — appliances, slides, leveling systems, water heater, LP system, etc.

**Examples:** Propane regulator malfunction, slide seal not seating properly, refrigerator not cooling to temperature, awning motor not operating.

**Key requirement:** PDI must be completed before the unit leaves the lot. Performing it after delivery may invalidate the claim.

## Warranty

**When to use:** When a customer reports a defect or malfunction during the manufacturer's warranty period.

This is the most common claim type. The customer brings in their unit or calls with a complaint, you diagnose the issue, and file a warranty claim for the repair labor and parts.

**Examples:** Delamination, slide mechanism failure, roof leak, electrical system faults, plumbing leaks.

**Warranty period:** Varies by manufacturer. Jayco, Forest River, Heartland, Keystone, Columbia NW, and Midwest Auto each have specific warranty terms. Check the unit's warranty documentation.

## Extended Warranty

**When to use:** When the manufacturer's warranty has expired, but the customer purchased an aftermarket extended service plan (ESP).

Extended warranty claims go to the ESP provider, not the manufacturer. Processing timelines and FRC code requirements may differ from standard warranty claims.

**Examples:** Any mechanical or structural failure that would normally be covered by OEM warranty, after the OEM period has ended.

## Insurance

**When to use:** When the damage was caused by a covered incident — not a manufacturing defect.

Insurance claims go through the customer's RV insurance provider. You'll need policy information and may need to complete a damage estimate report.

**Examples:** Storm damage (hail, wind, falling trees), collision damage, water flood damage, fire, theft.

**Note:** Insurance claims typically require photos of the damage taken immediately after the incident, before any repairs begin.
