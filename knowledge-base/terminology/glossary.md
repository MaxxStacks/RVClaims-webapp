# DealerSuite360 Glossary

A reference guide to the terms used throughout DealerSuite360 and the RV warranty industry.

## VIN — Vehicle Identification Number
A unique 17-character code assigned to every motor vehicle and RV at the factory. The VIN is the primary tracking key for all units and claims in DealerSuite360. Found on the dashboard, driver's side door jamb sticker, and title documents.

## FRC — Flat Rate Code
A manufacturer-specific code that identifies a type of repair and the standard labor hours (flat rate) allocated for it. FRC codes are not universal — each manufacturer has their own code system. DealerSuite360 maintains a database of FRC codes for all supported manufacturers.

## 3C Description — Complaint, Cause, Correct
The industry-standard format for describing warranty repairs:
- **Complaint** — What the customer reported or what was found during inspection
- **Cause** — The root cause of the problem identified by the technician
- **Correct** — The repair action performed to fix the issue

All FRC lines in a claim require a 3C description.

## DAF — Dealer Authorization Form
A claim type filed when a new unit arrives at the dealership from the manufacturer. Documents any transit damage or factory defects found upon delivery. Must be filed promptly (usually within 30 days of receipt).

## PDI — Pre-Delivery Inspection
A claim type filed before a unit is delivered to a customer. Documents any defects or system failures discovered during the final inspection process.

## GAP — Guaranteed Asset Protection
An insurance product that covers the difference between what a customer owes on their RV loan and the actual cash value of the vehicle in the event of a total loss. Sold through the F&I module.

## F&I — Finance and Insurance
The department (or service) responsible for arranging customer financing and selling insurance and protection products (GAP, extended warranty, protection packages) at point of sale.

## Preauth# — Pre-Authorization Number
A number issued by the manufacturer approving a repair before it is performed. Required for warranty repairs. The operator logs this number in your claim once received from the manufacturer.

## Claim# — Manufacturer Claim Number
The tracking number assigned by the manufacturer to a submitted claim. Used for all follow-up communication with the manufacturer. The operator logs this number in your claim.

## Unit
An RV in your dealership's inventory. Could be a Travel Trailer, Fifth Wheel, Class A or C motorhome, Van Camper, etc. Each unit is identified by its VIN.

## Client
A customer who has purchased an RV from your dealership. Clients can have access to the Customer Portal to track their unit's warranty and service history.

## Dealer
A dealership that subscribes to DealerSuite360 to manage their warranty claims and business operations.

## Operator
The DealerSuite360 team. Operators process claims on the manufacturer's portal on your behalf, review photos, assign FRC codes, and manage your account.

## Module
A feature bundle within DealerSuite360. The base subscription includes Claims Processing (Module 1). Additional modules (Financing, F&I, Parts, Marketing, Marketplace) can be activated separately.

## Subscription
Your monthly agreement with DealerSuite360. Two types: Plan A (monthly fee) and Plan B (pre-funded wallet).

## Tier / Plan
Plan A (subscription-based) or Plan B (wallet/pre-funded). Plan tier affects your per-claim processing fees and billing structure.

## ETA / ETD
Estimated Time of Arrival / Departure. Used when tracking parts orders and delivery timelines.

## ESP — Extended Service Plan
An aftermarket warranty product purchased separately from the OEM manufacturer's warranty. Covers repairs after the standard warranty period expires.
